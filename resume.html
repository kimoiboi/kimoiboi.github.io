Profile

During my initial year of college, I started out as a biology major, hoping to become an oncologist and fully expecting to enjoy the field. As time passed, I realized it wasn’t the industry I wanted to pursue since I began dreading about the long years of schooling and how little the work actually excited me day to day. After spending a lot of time researching the different majors and programs offered while reflecting on what I enjoy, I found myself becoming more and more drawn to Computer Science.

I loved the idea of understanding how systems work on a deeper level, strengthening my abstract thinking, and literally giving instructions to a computer to make it do something I imagined. The more I explored it, the more it felt like being a kid in a candy store. That curiosity ultimately pushed me to switch my major to Applied Computer Science with a focus in Software Engineering, and it’s been one of the best decisions I’ve made.

Since then, I’ve especially developed an interest in embedded programming, where I get to combine low-level hardware interaction with software design. It’s the perfect blend of challenge and creativity, and it feels aligned with both my curiosity and the kind of long-term career I want to build.
